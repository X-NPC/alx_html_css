Atomic (bombs) Habits

1. Make it obvious

The first step is to make the cues for your desired habit obvious. This means surrounding yourself with reminders of your goal and making it easy to get started. For example, if you want to start exercising, you can leave your workout clothes out the night before or put a sticky note on your bathroom mirror.

1. Make it attractive

The next step is to make the behavior itself attractive. This means finding ways to make the habit enjoyable or rewarding. For example, if you want to start eating healthier, you can focus on finding healthy foods that you actually enjoy.

2. Make it easy

Once you've made the cue and the behavior itself attractive, you need to make it easy to actually do the habit. This means removing any obstacles that might get in your way. For example, if you want to start waking up early, you can set your alarm clock across the room so that you have to get out of bed to turn it off.

3. Make it satisfying

The final step is to make the habit satisfying. This means finding ways to reward yourself for sticking with your goal. For example, if you want to start saving money, you can treat yourself to a new outfit after you've reached a certain savings goal.

Clear argues that by following these four steps, you can create new habits that will stick in the long term. He also provides a number of tips and tricks for overcoming common challenges, such as procrastination and temptation.
